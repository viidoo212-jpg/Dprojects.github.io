MEDITRACK Operations, Patients and Financials Analysis

The MEDITRACK analytical system consists of three core dashboards—Operations, Patients, and Financials—providing a complete, data-driven view of hospital network performance. Together, they help leadership monitor efficiency, patient outcomes, and financial health across multiple hospitals and departments. OPERATION Dashboard Purpose To provide insight into hospital capacity, workflow efficiency, and overall operational performance. Key Insights, 4,000 total admissions, showing strong patient volume across the network. Average wait time: 5.65 minutes/hours (depending on metric), indicating overall moderate efficiency. Bed occupancy rate: 75.33%, suggesting most facilities have stable utilization without being overwhelmed. 51 hospitals tracked within the MEDITRACK system. 40 patients per doctor, indicating average clinical workload. Operational Trends, Monthly comparison of 2023 vs 2024 admissions shows fluctuations, with noticeable dips and peaks that can guide staffing and planning. Geographical admission distribution reveals key regions driving patient activity. Department-level analysis shows consistent demand across major specialties like General Surgery, Cardiology, and Pediatrics. Hospital-level rankings highlight top-performing facilities in patient admissions.

PATIENTS DASHBOARD

Purpose, To assess clinical outcomes, service quality, and patient demographic distribution across the network. Key Insights, 2,000 total patients recorded within the reporting period. 74.48% recovery rate, indicating strong overall patient outcomes. 3.43% mortality rate, consistent with expected clinical benchmarks. 9.75% readmission rate, useful for identifying areas of care requiring improvement. Monthly Patient Metrics, Each month is evaluated across: Mortality rates Readmission rates Recovery outcomes Patient volumes Average wait times Some months, such as February, show higher readmission and mortality, potentially signaling seasonal or resourcing factors. Department & Demographic Insights, Recovery and mortality vary significantly by department, with Maternity, Pediatrics, and General Medicine demonstrating the strongest outcomes. Gender distribution is nearly even, with 49.62% female and 50.38% male patients. Hospital-specific charts show patient contribution from each location.

FINANCIALS DASHBOARD

Purpose, To evaluate the financial performance of the healthcare network, including costs, revenue, profit, and reimbursement efficiency. Key Insights, Total cost: $34.39M Total profit: $2.74M Profit margin: 7.39%, reflecting narrow but stable financial performance. Total billings: $40.34M Reimbursement rate: 92.06%, indicating effective claims processing. Outstanding claims: $3.20M, offering an opportunity for improved recovery. Financial Trends, Monthly sales comparison for 2023 vs 2024 shows peaks during months like March and October. The global map highlights regions generating the most profit. Cost & Profitability Breakdown, Costs are split by year, hospital, and department, revealing: Highest-cost hospitals (e.g., King’s College, Chelsea & Westminster). High-cost departments such as Oncology, Neurology, and Cardiology. Profitability rankings show the top-performing hospitals financially, enabling strategic resource allocation.
